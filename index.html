Phase 1
Business & SEO Positioning
🏗️

Business Model

B2B construction materials procurement intermediary. Almabsat acts as a sourcing agent — not a seller — connecting Saudi contractors and developers with verified material suppliers via RFQ and BOQ-based quotation flows.

👥

Primary Users

Saudi contractors, sub-contractors, project managers, and procurement officers managing residential and commercial construction. Secondary: real estate developers managing multiple concurrent BOQs.

🎯

Core Conversion Actions

1) RFQ form submission per material category, 2) BOQ file upload (/upload-boq.html), 3) WhatsApp inquiry. All three paths lead to offline quotation. There is no self-serve checkout or instant pricing.

💰

Revenue Logic

Lead generation + supplier matching margin. Almabsat earns on the spread between supplier cost and buyer quotation, similar to a managed marketplace model. SEO value = qualified lead volume × conversion rate × margin per order.

📦

Product Catalogue

Cement (Portland, finishing, white, sulfate-resistant), Sand & Gravel (multiple grades), Concrete Blocks (10–30cm sizes, insulated, volcanic). +150 SKUs claimed. Only 3 category pages exist in the sitemap.

🗺️

SEO's Role

SEO must target procurement-intent queries where buyers are actively sourcing materials. Every organic landing = potential RFQ. The site currently has near-zero organic visibility. SEO is the highest-ROI acquisition channel available to Almabsat at this stage.

🇸🇦

Market Context

Saudi Arabia is running trillion-dollar infrastructure programs (NEOM, Red Sea Project, Vision 2030). Construction materials procurement is fragmented and manual. Digital procurement is in early adoption — first-mover SEO advantage is massive and still available.

Phase 6
Saudi & Arabic SEO Strategy

🇸🇦 Arabic SEO Reality Check

Saudi contractors search predominantly in Arabic. Procurement managers who are Saudi nationals use Arabic search terms. The English version of the site is secondary — it serves expat engineers, non-Arabic-speaking procurement officers, and serves as a brand signal. The Arabic version must be the SEO priority, optimized for authentic Saudi dialect and procurement vocabulary — not translated English.

SERP Competition in Saudi Construction Keywords

Key opportunity: Many Saudi construction procurement queries have surprisingly low SERP competition from Arabic-language content. Most results are dominated by general Arabic content sites, directories, or BRKZ. A well-structured content strategy can achieve top-5 positions for long-tail procurement queries within 60–90 days due to low competition in this niche.
SASO & Saudi Standards Content Angle: Saudi contractors frequently look for materials that comply with SASO (Saudi Standards, Metrology and Quality Organization) specifications. Content targeting "اسمنت معتمد SASO" or "مواصفات SASO للبلك الاسمنتي" has near-zero competition and very high procurement intent. Almabsat's supplier certification focus is a natural fit for this content.

Phase 8
UX + SEO Conversion Optimization
UX/CRO Issue Severity SEO + Lead Impact Fix
WhatsApp as primary conversion path High WhatsApp clicks are not trackable in Google Analytics / Search Console. Almabsat cannot measure SEO → conversion attribution. No way to know which organic pages generate leads. Cannot optimize. Implement UTM parameters on WhatsApp links. Add on-site RFQ forms with Google Analytics event tracking. Tag WhatsApp as a goal. Build conversion-measurement infrastructure now before scaling SEO.
No pricing signals anywhere on site High Procurement officers won't fill an RFQ without price anchoring. "Get a quote" without any price context has significantly lower conversion vs. "From X SAR/ton — get exact pricing." Creates bounce from comparison shoppers. Add "starting from" pricing or price range indicators per category (even if not exact). Example: "اسمنت بورتلاندي — من ٢٠ ريال/كيس — اطلب عرض سعر دقيق". This alone can lift RFQ conversion rate significantly.
BOQ upload page likely thin on content High The /upload-boq page is potentially the highest-converting page on the site (project-ready buyer, defined need, clear intent). If it's just a file upload form with no content, it: cannot rank organically, offers no trust signals, has no FAQ to reduce upload hesitation. Expand /upload-boq with: accepted file formats, what happens after upload (SLA), sample BOQ template download (lead magnet), FAQ section (what is BOQ, how to prepare it), testimonial/social proof. Add FAQ schema.
No trust signals (certifications, photos, supplier names) High Procurement managers placing bulk orders (SAR 50K–500K+) need trust validation. No visible supplier names, no delivery photos, no certifications visible, no project testimonials. High-value buyers bounce to BRKZ which shows verified suppliers and pricing. Add: "موردون معتمدون" section with supplier logos (even anonymized as "factory partner"), delivery photos, SASO compliance badges, order counter ("١٢٠٠+ طلب مكتمل"). These are also on-page content that improve SEO relevance.
Mobile-first UX for field contractors Medium Saudi contractors access procurement information from the job site on mobile. If the RFQ form requires scrolling, typing, or uploading on mobile — friction kills conversions. WhatsApp CTA may be the right answer here for mobile-first users (it's fast), but only if tracked. Test entire RFQ flow on mobile. Add prominent mobile-optimized WhatsApp floating button. Ensure BOQ upload works from mobile (camera → PDF). Consider "اتصل بنا مباشرة" as third CTA for mobile users who prefer voice.
CTA placement not optimized for scroll depth Medium On the materials page, RFQ CTAs appear after each product section — good. But on the homepage, the CTA ("اطلب عرض سعر") only appears at top and bottom. Mid-page users who scroll through "How It Works" and "Why Almabsat" sections have no conversion exit point. Add inline RFQ CTA between every 2–3 homepage sections. Test sticky "اطلب الآن" button on mobile. A/B test CTA copy: "اطلب عرض سعر" vs. "احصل على سعر اليوم" vs. "أرسل متطلباتك".
No quantity calculator or estimation tool Medium A quantity calculator ("كم أحتاج من الاسمنت؟") is a high-dwell-time feature that builds SEO signals, reduces pre-purchase uncertainty, and naturally leads users toward an RFQ. BRKZ does not appear to have this — it would be a Almabsat differentiator. Build a simple interactive cement/block quantity calculator. Input: area (m²), floor type → Output: estimated cement bags / blocks needed → CTA: "اطلب الكمية المحسوبة — عرض سعر". This page alone can rank for "حاسبة اسمنت" queries.
No remarketing pixel or intent capture Low Organic visitors who don't convert on first visit are lost forever without a remarketing mechanism. B2B procurement has long consideration cycles — buyers may visit 3–5 times before committing to an RFQ. Install Meta Pixel + Google Tag Manager. Build remarketing audience of /materials page visitors. Run low-budget Snapchat/Google retargeting (Saudi contractors are heavy Snapchat users). Cost-per-lead will be very low for retargeted audiences.
